## The heaving-line / painter length finding (resolved, not silently)

The task asked this reconciliation to specifically resolve, where
possible, "heaving-line number and use." This is now resolved with high
confidence, **but not changed in any live content by this task**:

**Finding:** the live question `q-quiz1a-heaving-line-length` ("How long
is the heaving line on a liferaft? A: 100 feet") is very likely testing a
fact that actually belongs to the **painter/operating cord**, not the
heaving line specifically.

**Evidence:**
1. Slide 16's diagram labels the coiled rope **"100' Sea Painter/
   Operating Cord"** and, in a second close-up photo on the same slide,
   **"100 ft painter operating cord"** — twice, independently, on one
   authoritative class-deck slide.
2. Slide 25 states in running text: **"The operating cord has a length
   of at least 100 ft"** — a third, independent restatement.
3. Nowhere in the class deck, the CFR extraction from the prior readiness
   audit, or any other source found in this project is the **heaving
   line's** length ever stated as 100 feet. CFR §160.151-21(a) confirms
   only the heaving line's breaking strength (≥1.1 kN/250 lb) and
   attachment point (near the entrance furthest from the painter) — never
   a length figure.
4. The heaving line and the painter/operating cord are explicitly
   different lines in the same diagram (slide 16, 22, 36 all show both
   "Heaving Line" and "Operating Cord (Painter)" as separate labeled
   items) — they are not alternate names for the same rope.

**Conclusion:** the evidence strongly suggests `q-quiz1a-heaving-line-length`
mislabels a painter/operating-cord fact as a heaving-line fact. This is
not changed here — per instruction, no live question was edited in this
task — but it is carried into the re-audit (Part 6) as a concrete
"needs answer correction" finding, and a corrected candidate question
(painter/operating-cord length, not heaving-line length) is included in
the staged candidate pack.

Separately, the **painter length formula for rigid liferafts** (10m +
stowage height, or 15m, whichever is greater — slide 25) is a distinct,
more complex fact with no existing live coverage at all, and is a strong
new-candidate source in its own right.

**A second, previously-undetected confusion risk was found while
duplicate-checking the staged candidates**: the already-live
`q-quiz3-seapainter-length` (chapter-2-using-lifeboats) tests a
**lifeboat's** sea painter length ("at least twice the distance between
the boat deck and the waterline when light") — a completely different
formula, for a completely different craft, from the liferaft's painter
covered here. The two facts don't conflict (they're genuinely different
requirements for genuinely different equipment), but "sea painter length"
now exists as a compare-and-contrast trap across two chapters and should
be flagged as a commonly-confused-concept once Chapter 3 material is
promoted, the same way this project already handles other
lifeboat-vs-liferaft terminology pairs.
