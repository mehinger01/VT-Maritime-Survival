# Chapter 4 Targeted Drill Sets (Recommended Groupings of Existing Questions)

**Status: drill-set recommendations only. Every question ID below already
exists live in `src/content/course.json` — no question was created,
edited, or promoted to produce this document. These are suggested
groupings for focused practice, not a new content type or a change to the
app's data model, matching the same convention established in
`vinci-chapter-5-drill-sets.md` and `vinci-chapter-6-drill-sets.md`.**

Each drill set below groups existing Chapter 4 question IDs around one
specific area of the manual, so a student (or the app, if this pattern is
ever wired into a "drill mode" UI) can practice just that area in a
focused round rather than the full 75-question chapter pool.

---

## 1. Cold Water Survival & Hypothermia

**Purpose:** The single richest and most tested area of Chapter 4 — this
drill covers the full progression from the first-minute Cold Shock
Response through hypothermia's clinical stages, symptoms, and first aid,
matching the "Cold Water Survival — Hypothermia & Cold Shock Response"
Learn Mode module almost one-to-one.

**Related concepts:** the 4-stage cold-water danger progression (Cold
Shock Response, Cold Incapacitation, Circum-Rescue Collapse, Hypothermia);
the 70°F water-temperature threshold; air-temperature vs. water-temperature
hypothermia risk; why shivering that stops is more dangerous than shivering
that continues; rectal vs. oral temperature reliability; gentle-handling
and rewarming-priority first aid; the H.E.L.P. position.

**Recommended existing question IDs:**
`q-surv-1`, `q-surv-5`, `q-surv-6`, `q-surv-7`, `q-surv-9`, `q-surv-10`,
`q-quiz1a-hypothermia-water-temp`, `q-ch4man-airtemp-vs-watertemp`,
`q-ch4man-alcohol-hypothermia`, `q-ch4man-coldwater-swim-exhaustion`,
`q-ch4man-coldincapacitation-stage`, `q-ch4man-circumrescue-collapse`,
`q-ch4man-hypothermia-drunk-appearance`, `q-ch4man-rectal-vs-oral-temp`,
`q-ch4man-lowest-recovery-temp`, `q-ch4man-gentle-handling-heart`,
`q-ch4man-rewarming-priority-areas`, `q-ch4man-help-position-purpose`

**Misconception this drill corrects:** Treating "cold water is dangerous"
as one single problem instead of a sequence of distinct dangers on
different timescales — a question about the first minute (Cold Shock
Response) needs a different answer than one about hypothermia proper, even
though both are "cold water dangers." This is also the exact distinction
`q-surv-1` was corrected to test cleanly (see the companion
promotion-review document for that correction's details).

## 2. Abandoning Ship & Water Entry

**Purpose:** Groups the protective-equipment decisions (PFD vs. immersion
suit vs. Anti-Exposure Suit) with the water-entry procedure itself — the
sequence of choices a survivor makes between the order to abandon ship and
being in the water.

**Related concepts:** PFD required color and self-righting property vs.
an immersion suit's lack of one; why a PFD and immersion suit together can
be inappropriate (buoyancy pillow conflict); the Anti-Exposure Suit's
mobility-for-protection tradeoff, its pockets/gear, and its alternate
names vs. the immersion suit's own nicknames; general jump-height guidance
vs. the immersion suit's separate CFR-sourced jump-height design spec; the
5-step stepping-off sequence; windward bow/stern entry through burning
oil; floating and flotation techniques (trapped-air, back-float, shirt,
pants) without a PFD.

**Recommended existing question IDs:**
`q-surv-2`, `q-ch4man-pfd-required-color`, `q-ch4man-pfd-vs-suit-selfright`,
`q-ch4man-suit-buoyancy-pillow-pfd`, `q-ch4man-antiexposure-tradeoff`,
`q-ch4man-antiexposure-names-usage`, `q-ch4man-antiexposure-pockets`,
`q-ch4man-jumpheight-upperbound`, `q-quiz1a-preferred-jump-height`,
`q-quiz6-abandonship-jump-procedure`, `q-ch4man-burningoil-swimtechnique`,
`q-quiz6-oilfire-waterentry`, `q-ch4man-float-no-pfd-methods`,
`q-ch4man-shirt-flotation`, `q-ch4man-pants-flotation`

**Misconception this drill corrects:** Treating "PFD," "immersion suit,"
and "Anti-Exposure Suit" as interchangeable protective gear. Each has a
specific purpose, a specific way it's worn (alone, or with a PFD), and a
specific tradeoff — mixing them up (e.g., wearing a PFD with a suit that
has a buoyancy pillow) is a real, tested scenario, not just vocabulary.

## 3. Man Overboard & Swimming for Survival

**Purpose:** Isolates the specific, different situation of one person
unexpectedly in the water while the ship is still underway, plus the
swimming techniques that situation and general survival swimming both
depend on.

**Related concepts:** why swimming after your own ship is the wrong
response; the crew's immediate response sequence; why the stern is turned
away from the person; the Williamson Turn's directional purpose (without
its flawed degree arithmetic — deliberately excluded, see the
promotion-review document); swimming at an angle across a strong current;
wave-slap airway protection; body-surfing small waves vs. diving under
large ones; the scissors-kick-plus-hand-sculling tread-and-scull
technique.

**Recommended existing question IDs:**
`q-ch4man-mob-causes`, `q-ch4man-mob-crew-response`,
`q-ch4man-mob-stern-away`, `q-ch4man-williamsonturn-purpose`,
`q-quiz1a-manoverboard-dont-swim`, `q-ch4man-waveslap-protection`,
`q-ch4man-bodysurf-vs-diveunder`, `q-quiz6-swim-strongcurrent-45deg`,
`q-ch4man-tread-scull-technique`

**Misconception this drill corrects:** "Do something active" is not
automatically the safe answer — several questions in this set pair an
active-sounding wrong answer (swim after the ship, fight a current
head-on) with a calmer, more passive or angled correct one. More effort
isn't the same as more correct.

## 4. Survival Priorities, Rationing & Morale

**Purpose:** The broadest cluster in Chapter 4 by design — it aggregates
everything about sustaining a group over an unknown wait: who leads, how
water and food are rationed and safely supplemented, how the craft's
environment is managed, and why morale is a real survival factor rather
than a comfort issue. This mirrors how the manual itself groups
Organizing for Survival, Survival on the Sea, Drinking Water, Food, and
several smaller sections into one overall "sustaining the group" theme.

**Related concepts:** command order and assigned craft roles; capsize
recovery (lifeboat vs. liferaft) and heavy-seas heading; seasickness and
fluid preservation; urination timing; survival-without-water figures and
the 1-pint-per-day rationing schedule; rain and condensation collection;
never drinking seawater; eating without water; safe vs. unsafe ice;
poisonous near-shore fish, turtle-handling caution, and bird-catching
methods; cold- and hot-environment tactics inside the craft; the official
lifeboat water-stowage requirement; who can receive water in the first 24
hours.

**Recommended existing question IDs:**
`q-ch4man-command-order`, `q-ch4man-survivalcraft-roles`,
`q-ch4man-capsize-recovery-craft`, `q-ch4man-heavyseas-heading`,
`q-ch4man-seasickness-preserve-fluids`, `q-ch4man-urinate-timing`,
`q-ch4man-survival-without-water-figures`, `q-ch4man-rationing-schedule`,
`q-ch4man-never-drink-seawater`, `q-ch4man-no-food-without-water`,
`q-ch4man-seaice-vs-saltice`, `q-ch4man-raincollection-methods`,
`q-ch4man-poisonous-nearshore-fish`, `q-ch4man-turtle-caution`,
`q-ch4man-bird-catching`, `q-ch4man-cold-environment-tactics`,
`q-ch4man-hot-environment-tactics`, `q-ch4-waterrequirement-official`,
`q-quiz1a-first-water-liferaft`, `q-surv-3`, `q-surv-4`, `q-surv-8`,
`q-surv-11`, `q-surv-12`

**Misconception this drill corrects:** Assuming survival priorities are a
flat, undifferentiated list, or that rationing/discipline can flex once
"things feel okay for now." Every sub-area here has a specific rule
(timing, exception, or technique) that exists precisely because the
intuitive reaction — eat/drink more when uncertain, skip rationing once
comfortable, treat morale as separate from safety — is the wrong one.

## 5. Beaching & Land-Finding

**Purpose:** Covers reaching land safely — recognizing the signs land is
near, and the specific surf-landing technique for each type of survival
craft.

**Note on naming:** the plan for this cycle originally proposed
"Beaching, Land-Finding & Survival Ashore" as the fifth drill set's name.
**Survival Ashore has zero live questions** — its Learn Mode module
remains explicitly marked incomplete pending the still-missing manual
page ~118 (see the companion promotion-review document) — so this set is
named more narrowly, **Beaching & Land-Finding**, to match what's actually
live rather than implying Survival Ashore coverage that doesn't exist yet.
If the missing page is obtained and Survival Ashore is later built out,
this set can be renamed and extended at that point.

**Related concepts:** cumulus cloud formation as a sign of nearby land;
the daytime onshore sea-breeze pattern and how to time a raft landing
around it; why a surf landing should be avoided unless necessary; the
pulling-boat (rowed) stern-first approach with the sweep rigged and rudder
stowed; the power-boat approach (engine off, oars and sweep, same
stern-first positioning); the liferaft-specific night-landing and
rocks/coral puncture caution; what to do with a liferaft once ashore.

**Recommended existing question IDs:**
`q-ch4man-avoid-surf-landing`, `q-ch4man-beaching-powerboat-approach`,
`q-ch4man-beaching-pullingboat-approach`, `q-ch4man-raft-beaching-caution`,
`q-ch4man-cumulus-clouds-land`, `q-ch4man-seabreeze-timing`,
`q-quiz6-beach-landing-hightideline`

**Misconception this drill corrects:** Assuming a survival craft's surf
approach uses the same steering method as normal operation. Both the
pulling boat and the power boat deliberately set the rudder aside in favor
of oars and the sweep — an instinct to "steer normally" is exactly the
wrong move this drill trains against.

## Notes for whoever wires this into the app (future task, not this one)

- These groupings are pure metadata recommendations — they don't require
  any change to the question schema. If a future "drill mode" feature is
  built, each drill set here could become a filter (`ids: [...]`) with no
  new question content needed.
- **2 live Chapter 4 questions aren't part of any of the five drill sets
  above** — `q-ch4man-amver-purpose` and `q-ch4man-stay-near-position`
  (both from the small "Understanding Coast Guard Searches" section).
  They didn't naturally fit one of the five named groupings, the same
  precedent set in `vinci-chapter-6-drill-sets.md` for questions outside
  its five named clusters.
- Drill Set 4 (Survival Priorities, Rationing & Morale) is intentionally
  the largest and most heterogeneous set, since it's the one explicitly
  named in the plan for this cycle and the manual itself treats this as
  one broad "sustaining the group" theme spanning several Learn Mode
  modules. A future task could split it further (e.g., a dedicated
  Food & Water Safety set) if a narrower grouping is wanted later.
