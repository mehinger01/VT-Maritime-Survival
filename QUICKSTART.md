# QUICKSTART — Maritime Survival Study Tool

Hi Vinci — this is your study tool for the maritime/water survival exam.
Here's what it is and how to use it tonight.

## 1. What this is

**Maritime Survival Study Tool** is a study companion built specifically
for your exam prep. It's designed to help you:

- Learn from your actual course material, topic by topic
- Practice with retrieval-based questions (not just re-reading)
- Track your mastery of each topic over time
- Spot exactly what's confusing so you know what to ask your instructor

It runs entirely in your browser — nothing is uploaded anywhere, and your
progress is saved on this device.

**Important up front:** the questions in the app right now are
**draft placeholders**, not verified exam content. See section 7 below
before you trust any specific question or answer.

## 2. What to try first (tonight)

Go through these in order, just to get a feel for the tool:

1. **Course Home** — your dashboard: overall readiness and mastery by topic.
2. **Topic Explorer** — browse the topics and see what's covered.
3. **Learn Mode** — pick one topic and read through it.
4. **Quiz Mode** — do a short set of questions (8 questions, mixed topics).
5. **Flag anything confusing** — there's a "Flag for later review" button
   on every question. Use it freely tonight.
6. **Session Report** — check this at the end to see how you did.

That's it for the first pass. Don't worry about "getting it right" —
tonight is about testing the tool and telling us what's confusing.

## 3. How to study with it, day to day

Once you're using it regularly, here's a simple routine:

- **20–30 minute blocks.** Short and focused beats long and tired.
- **Start with your weakest topics** (Course Home shows you which ones).
- **Answer honestly on confidence.** When it asks "how sure were you?",
  answer for real — even "guessing" is useful information, not a bad grade.
- **Review mistakes before new questions.** Check Review Mistakes first
  each session — it's easier to fix something recent than something old.
- **Use Drill Mode for weak spots.** It's harder on purpose (no hints) and
  it automatically pulls from the topics you're struggling with.
- **Flag as you go.** Anything unclear, wrong-feeling, or worth asking
  your instructor — flag it in the moment, don't try to remember later.

## 4. What do the mastery/readiness scores actually mean?

Each topic's mastery percentage is based on:

- How many questions in that topic you've attempted
- Whether you got them right
- How confident you were
- Whether you've covered the whole topic or just a few questions

**These are estimates to guide your study time, not a prediction of your
exam score.** A 70% mastery doesn't mean a 70 on the real exam — it means
"you're about 70% of the way toward consistently answering this topic's
practice questions correctly." Use it to decide what to study next, not
as a grade.

## 5. How and when to flag a question

Flag a question any time you see:

- Wording that's confusing or ambiguous
- An answer that feels wrong, outdated, or doesn't match what you were taught
- Something that doesn't match your book, notes, or instructor's explanation
- Anything you'd want to double-check with your instructor before the exam

Flagged questions all show up in one place (**Flagged Questions**) so we
can review them together and fix the content.

## 6. Learning Journal (now built)

You asked for a spreadsheet-style record of your study sessions — dates,
times, and notes on what was covered. That's now a screen in the app:
**Learning Journal**, in the main navigation.

Each entry tracks:

- Session number, date, start/end time, and duration
- Topics covered and resources used
- Notes from the session
- Student action items and questions for the instructor
- Status: Planned, Completed, or Needs Follow-Up

This screen is **view-only for you** — Mike keeps it updated after each
tutoring session, so you can always check it but won't accidentally
change the record. There's an **Export CSV** button any time you or Mike
want the log open in Excel or Google Sheets — say, to share with an
instructor or keep as a record of tutoring hours.

## 7. What's placeholder content (read this before trusting any question)

Right now, all topics and questions in the app are **draft placeholders**.
They were written to get a working tool in front of you quickly, but they
have **not** been checked against your actual book, class notes, or
instructor's slides.

This means:

- Some questions may be too easy, too hard, or slightly off from how your
  instructor actually teaches it.
- Some explanations may be incomplete or generic.
- Topic coverage may not match your instructor's chapter breakdown exactly.

**Please don't treat any specific question or answer as verified exam
truth yet.** Flag anything that seems off — that's exactly how we'll fix
it. The tool's mechanics (quizzing, tracking, review) are solid; the
content is still being built out from your real material.
