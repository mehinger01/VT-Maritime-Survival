## 2026-07-15 — Chapter 5 Learn Mode & Study Material Expansion

**Scope:** Chapter 5 — Signaling to Attract Attention.

Chapter 5 study materials were expanded using Water Survival Student
Manual pages 120–132, focused on signaling to attract attention. Work
covered VHF radios, EPIRBs, SARTs, Morse code/SOS, shore and aircraft
signals, pyrotechnics, smoke, mirrors, whistles, radar reflectors, and
the international distress signals list.

**What was produced:**

- Expanded in-app Learn Mode content for Chapter 5 from 1 of 8 sections
  with real teaching content to 6 of 8, covering: the VHF Survival Craft
  Transceiver; a combined EPIRB-vs-SART comparison section addressing
  the chapter's most commonly confused device pair; International Morse
  Code / SOS; a combined "Signals to Communicate From Shore & Table of
  Lifesaving Signals" section; and a "Signaling Devices" section
  covering mirrors, parachute flares, hand flares, smoke signals, dye
  markers, whistles, and radar reflectors. Each section follows a
  consistent instructional structure: what the topic covers, why it
  matters in a real rescue, the key facts and figures, common
  student confusions, scenario-based guidance for choosing the right
  signal, and short memory anchors.
- A standalone student-facing comparison table (11 devices/signal types
  across purpose, best use case, key exam detail, and common confusion
  trap) for quick pre-quiz review.
- Six targeted drill-set groupings of existing, already-live Chapter 5
  questions (EPIRB vs. SART; VHF Channel 16 vs. Channel 6; visual vs.
  audio vs. electronic signals; smoke vs. dye marker vs. flare;
  distress-signal recognition; rescue aircraft/shore communication
  signals), each with a stated purpose and the specific misconception it
  targets.

**What did not change:** No new quiz questions were created or promoted
in this session. The live question count remains unchanged. No
unrelated chapters were touched. `q-ch5man-shore-apparatus-signals`
remains held, pending a source-image verification step from a prior
session, and was not promoted here.

**Source basis:** `vinci-2026-07-14-chapter-5-source-intake.md` (source
transcription) and `vinci-2026-07-14-chapter-5-promotion-review.md`
(question-bank status reference), both already staged from a prior
session; no new source extraction or OCR was performed in this session.
